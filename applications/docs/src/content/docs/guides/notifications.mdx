Reminders are Sideline's most-loved feature. They make sure nobody has to manually chase players for RSVPs.

## When reminders fire

By default, Sideline sends two rounds of reminders per event:

- **Pre-deadline** — N hours before the RSVP deadline, ping everyone who hasn't answered yet.
- **Post-deadline** — shortly after the deadline, ping remaining holdouts one last time.

The exact timing is configurable per team from **Team settings → Notifications**.

## Where reminders go

- **In Discord**, in the team's reminders channel (or events channel if no separate reminders channel is configured). Each reminder @-mentions every player who hasn't responded.
- Reminders are **one-off posts** — once everyone has answered, no more pings for that event.

## Who gets pinged

Only players in the event's **target group** who haven't RSVPed. Once someone clicks Yes / No / Maybe, they drop off the reminder list.

## Disabling reminders

Per-event: toggle **Reminders** off when creating or editing the event.

Per-team: from **Team settings → Notifications**, set **Reminders enabled** to off. Useful if you're running a one-off tournament with its own comms channel.

<Aside type="tip" title="Reminder hygiene">
  Reminders are effective when they're rare enough to matter. If you trigger manual reminders every hour, people tune them out. Trust the defaults.
</Aside>
